# What is the Agent Quotient AQ?

Agent Quotient (AQ) is a measure of an organization's capability to effectively deploy, manage, and scale AI agents across its operations. It quantifies how well a company integrates autonomous AI systems into workflows, decision-making processes, and value creation activities.

## What This Means in Practice

A high Agent Quotient indicates that a company has successfully embedded AI agents into core business functions, enabling automation of complex tasks, improved decision velocity, and scalable operations without proportional headcount increases. Organizations with strong AQ demonstrate mature governance frameworks, clear agent ownership models, and systematic approaches to measuring agent performance. Building AQ requires deliberate architectural choices, workforce adaptation, and continuous refinement of how humans and agents collaborate within the organization.

## How Do Companies Improve Their Agent Quotient?

Companies improve their AQ by adopting frameworks like the Agent-First Company (AFC) playbook and Output-First Architecture (OFA), which provide structured approaches to integrating AI agents into business operations. This involves identifying high-value use cases for agent deployment, establishing clear metrics for agent performance, and creating feedback loops that enable continuous agent improvement. Organizations must also invest in upskilling teams to work effectively alongside agents, developing what's known as "agent fluency" where employees understand when to delegate to agents versus when human judgment is essential.
